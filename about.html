ℹ️ About NSW PSN

A free, ad-free hub for keeping an eye on what's happening across New South Wales — built and run by an enthusiast, for the community.

What is NSW PSN?
Overview

NSW PSN (NSW Public Safety Network) is a single place to watch what's unfolding across New South Wales in real time — bush fires, traffic incidents, road hazards, power outages, weather warnings, beach conditions, and more — pulled together from official and public sources onto one live map, live feed, and searchable log.

It also ties together the things scanner and public-safety enthusiasts already use: a live radio feed, a pager message feed, and a Discord bot that pushes alerts straight into your server — so you don't have to juggle a dozen browser tabs and government websites to stay informed.

Everything is aggregated, cached briefly, and presented as-is. It's a monitoring and reference tool — not an emergency service. In a real emergency, always call 000 and follow official advice from the relevant agency.

Why I started this
The story

I started this to help the community. Information about what's happening across NSW is out there, but it's scattered everywhere — RFS on one site, Live Traffic on another, the Bureau somewhere else, power outages behind yet another map, and the scanner and pager feeds off on their own again. To get a real picture of what's going on near you, you'd need half a dozen tabs open, each refreshing on its own, none of them talking to each other.

So I built the thing I wished existed and made it free for everyone: one map and one feed that quietly pull all of it together, update themselves, and let you filter down to what you care about. The goal is simple — give the community, the volunteers, the scanner enthusiasts, and anyone who just wants to stay informed one clear, honest place to see what's happening, without ads and without a catch.

What it aims to do
Mission
  • Put it all in one place. Aggregate NSW public-safety data that's normally spread across many sites into one live, filterable view.
  • Keep it fast and current. Auto-refreshing data with sensible caching, so you're looking at what's happening now — not a page you have to keep reloading.
  • Stay free and open to everyone. No paywall, no account required to watch, no "premium" tier for the important bits.
  • Respect your privacy. No ads, no behavioural tracking, no selling data. (More on that below.)
  • Be honest about what it is. A best-effort monitoring tool built on public data — not an official emergency service, and never a replacement for 000 or agency advice.
Ad-free & independent
No ads

There are no ads here, and there never will be. No banners, no pop-ups, no sponsored pins, no newsletter nags, no "accept cookies to continue" walls.

Just as importantly, there's no hidden cost paid with your data:

  • No advertising or marketing trackers
  • No third-party analytics like Google Analytics or Facebook Pixel
  • No cross-site behavioural profiling or fingerprinting
  • Your data is never sold, rented, or traded

The project is funded and run privately, out of pocket, because it's useful — not because it's making money off you. The only "analytics" is a lightweight, privacy-respecting count of active viewers so the site can tune how often it refreshes. See the Privacy Policy for the full detail.

What's included
Features

The data brought together on the map, live feed and logs includes:

  • NSW Rural Fire Service (RFS) bush fire incidents & Fire Danger Ratings
  • Live Traffic NSW / Transport for NSW — incidents, roadwork, floods, fires & major events
  • Waze — crowdsourced road hazards, police reports & roadwork
  • Bureau of Meteorology (BOM) — warnings & radar
  • Power outages — Endeavour Energy, Ausgrid & Essential Energy
  • Beach conditions — Beachwatch & BeachSafe NSW
  • Traffic, aviation & fire-tower cameras
  • Emergency-related news feeds

Plus the tools built around it:

  • Live radio feed of publicly receivable, non-encrypted transmissions
  • Pager message feed
  • Discord bot for per-server alert subscriptions — add it to your server from the dashboard
  • Incident map editor for approved contributors
What's next
Plans

This is an evolving, actively-developed project. Things on the roadmap and under ongoing improvement include:

  • More data sources and better coverage across NSW
  • Smarter, more useful radio-traffic summaries
  • Continued performance and reliability work as usage grows
  • More control in the Discord bot and dashboard
  • Quality-of-life improvements based on community feedback

Ideas and feature requests are genuinely welcome — the best improvements have come from people who use it. Bring them to the Discord.
