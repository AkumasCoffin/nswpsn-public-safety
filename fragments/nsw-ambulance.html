🚑 NSW Ambulance – Overview
ASNSW
NSW Ambulance NSW Health

NSW Ambulance provides emergency medical response, aeromedical retrieval, rescue operations and non-emergency transport across more than 800,000 km² of New South Wales. Over 1.2 million incidents are handled yearly.

Coverage & Reach
  • Serves 7.9+ million people across metro, regional and remote NSW.
  • 250+ ambulance stations and response locations.
  • Road fleet travels ~40–45 million km per year.
  • One of the largest ambulance fleets in the world (around 1,500 vehicles).
Operational Divisions
  • Clinical Operations: front-line road paramedics and specialist roles.
  • Aeromedical Operations: helicopters & fixed-wing retrieval teams.
  • Special Operations Team (SOT) / RAA: remote & complex rescue environments.
  • Critical Care: CCPs, flight nurses & retrieval doctors.
Control Centres
  • Ambulance Control Centres in Sydney, Newcastle, Wollongong and Dubbo.
  • Aeromedical Control Centre for helicopter and fixed-wing dispatch.
  • CAD / MDT and GPS-based nearest resource assignment.
📟 Category Codes
CAT 1–4

NSW Ambulance category codes and whether they usually run lights and sirens.

Category Lights & Siren Description
CAT 1 Yes Patient is not breathing, unconscious, or in a serious time-critical condition.
CAT 2 Yes Patient is conscious and breathing but still serious and requires urgent response.
CAT 3 No Moderate condition requiring ambulance response but not lights & sirens.
CAT 4 No Minor / low acuity condition.

📡 Talkgroups & Monitoring Notes
NSW PSN
  • Each NSW Ambulance region has one or more primary dispatch talkgroups.
  • Aeromedical uses dedicated or shared retrieval channels.
  • Major incidents may link FRNSW, RFS, SES, Police on shared or patched channels.
  • Some talkgroups may be encrypted or carry limited traffic.
  • Traffic volume increases during storms, heatwaves and major events.
  • Do not publish identifiable patient information.
